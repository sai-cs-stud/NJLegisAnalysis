New Jersey County and Legislative District Data

About

Welcome to njcongdata.info. This website contains data that may or may not explain the current state of the New Jersey legislative districts. More specifically, our team has compiled data on the county and legislative district level(House of Representative districts) in order to investigate the possibility of Gerrymandering(more about Gerrymandering below). The data targeted for gathering included the indicators shown below. An indicator is a quantifiable variable our team decided early on in the data scraping process that may contribute to gerrymandering in New Jersey and thus was worthy enough to collect and perform a statistical analysis. All of the information, both at the county and legislative district level, was collected from public databases available to all. See our bibliography tab if you wish to examine where the data comes from.

What exactly is gerrymandering?

Gerrymandering is the process in which the ruling party of government reshapes voting boundaries to favor themselves in future elections. In the United States, every state in the union elects Representatives to represent the wishes of the people in specific geographic areas. These representatives have a duty to speak for their constituents in Washington D.C.(federal capital of the country). Each state has an allotted number of representatives based on the population of the state. How the districts are drawn up geographically within the state is up to the states themselves. Usually there is a neutral or bipartisan committee or commission tasked with this job. However, in some situations a state government’s majority party adulterates the process by forging districts that favor themselves to keep them in the majority. This can be done by making boundaries that put opposition voters in one district or decreasing the opposition’s chances across many districts.
